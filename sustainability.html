How We Make Better Happen

Our overarching strategy and commitment to sustainable solutions helps make a real, lasting impact on our business and those we serve.

Key indicators, measuring and reporting systems hold us accountable with goals laid out and defined in all facets of our business, especially for our emissions, energy and water usage and Better Packaging product solutions.

Emissions & Energy

We've set science-based targets to help limit global warming and lower greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions: reduce absolute Scope 1 and 2 emissions 25% by 2030 (from a 2020 baseline) and reduce absolute Scope 3 emissions 13.5% by 2030 (from a 2019 baseline).

To reach these goals, we invest in energy-efficient equipment, renewable energy sources and intermodal transport and work closely with suppliers to understand emissions impacts and accelerate Scope 3 reductions.

Water

We're working to reduce water usage at our paper mills by 3% by 2030 and 5% by 2040. We conduct water risk assessments at 100% of our mills, covering more than 90% of our global water usage, and implement process upgrades to reduce, reuse and better treat water across facilities.

Product Carbon Footprint

We use Life Cycle Assessments (LCAs) to measure and reduce the environmental impact of our products, from raw material sourcing to end-of-life. These insights help us design packaging with a lower carbon footprint and offer customers more sustainable, data-backed choices.

Sustainability Starts with Leadership

Scott Byrne, Our VP of Sustainability

Scott Byrne

Vice President, Global Sustainability at Sonoco

Scott Byrne serves as the Vice President of Global Sustainability at Sonoco, a global packaging solutions leader, committed to improving the lives of customers, teammates and communities. With his background in all aspects of environmental management and sustainability, Byrne helps ensure Sonoco is on the cutting edge of sustainability initiatives.

As the immediate past President of the American Institute for Packaging and the Environment (AMERIPEN) Board of Directors and current Board member, Byrne works to further the organization's goal of leading the packaging industry through advocacy based on science.

Prior to joining Sonoco, Byrne was the Market Sustainability Manager for the US and Canada at Tetra Pak, where he was responsible for the implementation of sustainability programs and driving positive stakeholder engagement as the Director of Government Affairs at the Carton Council of North America, a recycling-focused industry group.

Byrne holds a master's degree in environmental management and sustainability from the Stuart School of Business, Illinois Institute of Technology in Chicago, as well as a Juris Doctor from the Chicago-Kent College of Law, Illinois Institute of Technology. He is a member of the Florida Bar. Byrne received his bachelor's degree in business economics and public policy from Indiana University, Kelley School of Business, in Bloomington, Indiana. When not working, Scott enjoys exploring the outdoors with his wife, their two sons and their two miniature schnauzers.
